Video & Audio Production

Documentary

2024 – 2025

This collection brings together three documentary explorations of everyday artistry: a silent film portrait of a downtown café’s daily rhythm—from hushed morning prep through the bustling noon rush to the tranquil evening lull; Strings of Serenity, an intimate audio journey through nylon‑string hums, fingertip rhythms and whispered reflections on music as personal sanctuary; and a vibrant band documentary that weaves rehearsal drumbeats, interlocking guitar and keyboard melodies and candid backstage banter into a unified creative expression. Together, these works show how silence, sound and collaboration can reveal the extraordinary hidden in ordinary moments.

Silent Documentary - Downtown café shop

"This short, silent film documents a day in the life of a cozy café. From the quiet moments of the baristas getting ready for work in the early morning, to the rush noon and the transition to the laziness of the evening. The flow of people in the café converges to a microcosm of people’s socialize and interaction. The café hosts a variety of special moments, from the baristas’ detailed preparation work for every dessert and beverage to the customers that engage in the conversation. Until the end of the day, the café turns to a quiet and peaceful moment of the evening alone and carries the expectation of a next new day."

Audio Documentary - Strings of Serenity

“A guitar, a quiet room, and a mind at ease—how music becomes a personal sanctuary.”

"In a quiet room, he picks up his acoustic guitar. The soft vibrating sound of the strings, the beat sound of the finger strikes, the deep humming and breathing all make for a serenity scene. In this environment, playing has less to do with technique and more with feeling and emotional release. Through these natural soundscapes, recorded dialog and detailed field recordings, we will experience how music can be an extension of self‑expression and relaxation. As he strums away at the melody, they will reflect on why they play, how the music helps them relax, and what the guitar means to them."
